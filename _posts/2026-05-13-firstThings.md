---
title: "On Starting Something"
date: 2026-05-13
tags: [reciprocity]
---

I’ve been thinking about what it means to begin something in a way that feels honest. Not polished, not performative — just real enough to stand on its own. Most beginnings try too hard. They reach for certainty or some kind of authority no one actually has on day one.

This isn’t that. I haven't any certainty or authority.

This is a place where I think in the open without pretending to have the answers. A place where the work is shaped by intention rather than urgency and where ideas are allowed to be unfinished, because unfinished things are often the truest.

Better in Jeans is my attempt at that kind of space — unpretentious, a little rough around the edges in the way real things are. TWhere effort matters more than polish, and the return is shaped by the sincerity of the offering.  Not karma or luck. You give something real, and eventually something real finds its way back.

What goes around comes around, so this is me testing that equation.  
Giving a small offering, something honest, and seeing what comes back.

Here’s to beginnings that don’t need to be perfect to be worthwhile.
